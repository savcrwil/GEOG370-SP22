Displaying images of the world in different projections

Savannah Wilson

This is how I did it in QGIS:

I used a shapefile from naturalearth and the Tissot's Indicatrix plugin to create each of these projections. I set each map to a different projection and used the Indicatrix to model distortions caused by each projection.

WGS84 Mercator Projection

The Mercator projection is a classic! This is the one I am most familiar with. The Mercator projection conserves area, which is why the shape of regions near the poles is distorted.

WGS84 Pseudo-Mercator Projection

The Pseudo-Mercator projection creates a much longer map than other projections (some of it is cut off in the image). Land shape is conserved, but area becomes distorted closer to the poles.
